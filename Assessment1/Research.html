Copyright, Creative Common (CC) Licenses, Fair Use

4 Principles of Copyright in NZ

  • Originality: To be protected by copyright, a work must be original. This means it must originate from the author and not be a mere copy of someone else's work.
  • Automatic Protection: Copyright protection is automatic upon the creation of an original work. There is no need for registration or any formalities to obtain copyright.
  • Exclusive Rights: Copyright gives the creator exclusive rights to use, reproduce, distribute, perform, and display their work. These rights can be transferred or licensed to others.
  • Duration: Copyright does not last forever. In most jurisdictions, the protection lasts for the life of the author plus a certain number of years (e.g., 70 years in many countries). For works with multiple authors, it generally lasts for the life of the last surviving author plus 70 years.

    Web Security

    5 Common Cyber Attacks

    • Malware: Malicious software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system. Includes viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware.
    • Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Attempts to overwhelm a system, network, or website to make it unavailable to users, often by flooding it with traffic.
    • Phishing: Deceptive attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity, usually via email or fake websites.
    • Spoofing:Disguising communication from an unknown source as being from a known, trusted source. Can involve email, IP, or even website spoofing.
    • Identity-Based Attacks: Attempts to steal or misuse personal information for fraudulent purposes, often involving identity theft or unauthorized access to accounts.
